Newly Signed California Laws for Property Owners and Managers

Dear Property Owners and Managers,

We're sharing important updates on recent California legislation signed by Governor Newsom that impacts property management and tenant relations. These new laws aim to enhance tenant protections, streamline operations, and ensure compliance, supporting your efforts to manage properties effectively. Additionally, we've included reminders about critical inspection deadlines under SB 326 and SB 721 to help you stay compliant.

Key Legislative Updates:

Modernized Contract Cancellations: AB 1327 allows homeowners to cancel home improvement or solicitation contracts via email or phone, simplifying the process.

Tenant Protections and Property Management Enhancements:

AB 628: Starting in 2026, rental units must include a working stove and refrigerator to meet habitability standards.

AB 246: Protects tenants from eviction if Social Security benefit delays or reductions impact rent payments.

AB 414: Permits electronic return of security deposits with tenant consent, streamlining refund processes.

AB 391: Allows mobile home park notices to be delivered electronically with resident consent, improving communication efficiency.

AB 806: Prohibits mobile home parks from restricting residents' installation or use of air conditioning, enhancing tenant comfort.

AB 863: Directs the creation of a multilingual eviction summons form to improve language accessibility for tenants.

Compliance and Transparency: These laws promote fair practices, helping you maintain compliance and foster positive tenant relationships.

Reminders:

SB 326 (Condominiums): The deadline for initial balcony and exterior elevated element inspections was January 1, 2025. If you have not yet completed these inspections for your condominium properties, please take immediate action to ensure compliance with structural safety requirements. Contact a licensed structural engineer or inspector to address any outstanding obligations.

SB 721 (Apartment Buildings): The deadline for balcony and exterior elevated element inspections for apartment buildings is January 1, 2026. We strongly recommend scheduling these inspections early to ensure the safety of your tenants and compliance with state law. Consult a qualified professional to plan and complete these inspections.
